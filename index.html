Park of Maxim Gorky

Maxim Gorky Central Park for Culture and Recreation (Ukrainian: Парк культури і відпочинку «Максим
Горький», Russian: Центральный парк культуры и отдыха имени Максима Горького) is a Kharkiv, Ukraine,
city park consisting of over 130 hectares of land. It is bounded in the southern corner by Vesnina Street in the east
- Sumska Street to the north - the so-called elite private settlement construction, and in the west - Dynamo street
corner and Novgorod.

History

The origin of the park was from the City governing council who "put forth a proposition to plant trees on 20-40,000
square metres on both sides of the Sumskoy road" The idea was that the area would be used for horse riding in
a similar manner to the Boulogne Forest. Teachers, university students, and school students all assisted voluntarily
with the tree planting. Each tree had a plaque planted next to it showing who had planted it.
